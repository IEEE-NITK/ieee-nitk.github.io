Silicon Maze 2021

Date: 26th - 27th June 2021
SIG: CompSoc
Conducted in collaboration with Web Enthusiast's Club NITK, this was a 24 hour programming contest based on various disciplines of Computer Science like Data Structures and Algorithms, Systems, Machine Learning and Development. Themed around the Avengers, the 4 tracks were held on 3 different platforms: Algorithms on CodeDrills, Machine Learning on Kaggle, Systems and Development on HackerEarth, with equal weightage given to all tracks. Participants could take part in teams of three.

Resume Building Session

Date: 11th June 2021
SIG: All SIGs
An interactive session on how to build an outstanding resume, conducted for the first and second year student members who will soon be pursuing internship opportunities. The session covered what should and shouldn't be put on a resume, along with a walkthrough of some templates to get started. The session was conducted by Chandran N, final year executive member at IEEE NITK Student Branch.

Knowledge Sharing Session on Static Timing Analysis

Date: 05th - 06th July 2021
SIG: Diode
A Knowledge Sharing Session was conducted on Static Timing Analysis open to everyone to gain better insight into this topic through an informative interaction. The main session’s speaker was Hitesh S, an MTech student in Microelectronics and VLSI, pursuing at Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore. The session covered the basics of STA which includes Metastability in VLSI circuits, concepts of Setup and Hold time, Clock Skew, etc.
A separate pre-session was held at 6 PM on 5 July 2021 for gaining preliminary knowledge of the topic. This session was conducted by Rahul Hanchate, a final year ECE student at NITK.

Date: 24th - 25th July 2021

Summer Mentorship Program

Date: 06th June 2021 - 11th July 2021
SIG: All SIGs
This is a 4-week long summer mentorship project conducted by the sophomores of our club to help mentor the student members of Mangalore Sub-Section in their fields of interest. A total of 20 projects are being offered. Weekly tutorial meets will be held on different platforms such as Slack and Microsoft Teams, with reference materials continuously supplied to aid the mentees in their projects.
