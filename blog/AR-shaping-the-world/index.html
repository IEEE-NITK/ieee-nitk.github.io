    Augmented reality shaping the world

    What is AR (AUGMENTED REALITY)?

    In layman terms, we can say that it is superimposing of computer-generated image onto the real life view of the user. It is being used almost everywhere from zoos to military training.

    ### Why AR?

    AR is one of the fastest growing areas of research which will bring about a huge impact to the world, as AR is closely related to the ever fastest growing computer and technology world it will continue to grow, and we can expect a groundbreaking invention which could shake the world, like something shown bellow.

    AR and VR?

    When someone searches for AR there is another word that pops up with it, which is VR (VIRTUAL REALITY)

    So what is the difference between both of them

    • AR focuses on projecting a computer-generated image onto the screen of the view of the real world, for example, when we watch a cricket match which is a live feed at the bottom of the screen we can see the score and the names of the players and other information, even sometimes we can see the team logo floating above the ground, all of this is the simplest application of AR.

    • Now if we were to look at VR it is generating a 3-D virtual environment that can be interacted with in a real way with the help of some gadget, basically what happens here is that our vision is covered by a head gear with makes us feel like we are in a virtual environment and we feel like we are actually interacting with it.

    AR & Computer vision

    Computer vision and AR are closely related to each other, as computer vision is a field where it deals with computers making decisions based on visual information, for example the computer will ask a question like, “Is this a cat ?”, or “Is this a face ?”. In AR we take live feed and make changes to it, we can assume a live feed to be a continuously changing frame on which we can apply computer vision, this is how AR and computer vision are related. One more important thing to know is that a picture is considered as an 3-D array. Where we apply filters and do operations on. Now we will look at a way to detect a face in a certain picture and put a box around it.

    Face detection

    Before we can detect a face on a picture, there is a small concept that needs to be introduced which is called Haar transform, it is one of the simplest ways to do signal analysis.

    The above pic is the ` Haar wavelet`. One of the simplest applications of the 2-D haar transform is creating a sparse matrix (which are matrices which are having more 0) this is to make calculations easier (for example more zeros make it easier to calculate the determinant).

    Haar Cascades can be used for detecting edge feature, line feature, and four rectangle feature.

    The algorithm uses alternate dark and light rectangles to uniquely find a pattern of pixels that classify an object, in our case it would be to recognize a face. The sum of image intensities under the light rectangles is subtracted from the sum of intensities under the dark rectangles at each point in the image while sliding the kernel through the picture. As the algorithm progresses, more complex features are calculated to uniquely distinguish objects.

    Object detection for using haar cascade method was created by Paul Viola and Michael Jones in their paper, “Rapid Object Detection using a Boosted Cascade of Simple Features”, they have used ML to train their model and then they were able to get a very accurate model for the haar transform.

    Now there are many features that can tell us whether the picture has a face or not to be exact there are 160000+ features! This is a lot of features, but the lucky thing for us is that some properties are more important than the others, for example The first feature selected focuses on the property that the region of the eyes is often darker than the region of the nose and cheeks. The second feature selected relies on the property that the eyes are darker than the bridge of the nose. These are the most prominent features!

    So what Paul Viola and Michael Jones did was that they group every feature into groups and extended it so when a picture fails the first group it is discarded saying that it does not contain a face.

    now here is a small program to implement face recognition

    import numpy as np
import cv2 as cv
face_cascad=cv.CascadeClassifier('haarcascade_frontalface_default.xml)
img = cv.imread('tom_cruise.jpg',1)
gray = cv.cvtColor(img, cv.COLOR_BGR2GRAY)
faces = face_cascade.detectMultiScale(gray, 1.3, 5)
for (x,y,w,h) in faces:
    cv.rectangle(img,(x,y),(x+w,y+h),(255,0,0),2)
    roi_gray = gray[y:y+h, x:x+w]
    roi_color = img[y:y+h, x:x+w]
cv.imshow('img',img)
cv.waitKey()
cv.destroyAllWindows()

    The above program will give us an output which will put a box around the face like this

    AR in the world

    AR is used in many fields which include:

    • Medical
    • Military
    • Archaeology
    • Space research
    • Tourism

    The above mentioned area’s are the largest fields in which AR is used, now I would be talking about a certain device created in this field.

    This research was done with contact lenses incorporated with sensors to read the movements of the eye. This research was done in the Optics Department at IMT-Atlantique.

    As we can see in the above picture the contact lenses, these are fitted with sensors which will help with the analysis of the cognitive load which will help in measuring the blink, eye motion and directions. This contact lens will be fit into an AR helmet.

    The main components of these lenses are

    • First an optical which is devoted to the direction of sight extraction
    • One component for the RF communication and harvesting
    • One more component of the battery and the ASIC (integrated circuit, AN) that monitors all these functions.

    One of the greatest problems was making the battery which would be able to power the components of the lens. They used a very powerful stretchable self-healing Li-ion micro battery.

    Dont get the wrong idea these are not AR lenses but help in creating a AR interface, AR lenses are still a long way from becoming a reality.

    AR in Military

    The one place in the world where imagination beats reality, and the most amazing ideas can be seen to become true. Even before filters in snapshot or games like Pokémon-go came into the world, the military had the technology of AR and it is used for training of the soldiers in the ever changing techniques of warfare. If we had to interpret this in an easy way, it is like playing pubg in real life. Have you ever seen in movies, where in a fighter jet we see all the information on the plane’s stats being displayed on the pilot’s visor, well this is not just in the movies but also in real life. This is called Tactical Augmented reality (TAR). Research has been going on actively at U.S. Army Research, ` Development and Engineering Command’s Communications-Electronics Research `, Development and Engineering Center (CERDEC). The biggest improvement for soldiers is the fact that they don’t have to keep looking down at their GPS to know where they are, all the information is displayed on their helmet and this improves their overall awareness.
